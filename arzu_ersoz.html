About

Arzu Ersöz is Professor of Biochemistry at the Faculty of Science, Eskişehir Teknik Üniversitesi (ESTÜ), and CEO of Bionkit. She completed her B.Sc. and M.Sc. at ODTÜ (1986, 1988) and her Ph.D. in Analytical Chemistry at Hacettepe University (1998), followed by two postdoctoral fellowships at the University of Kentucky (1999–2001) on conducting polymers, screen-printing, laser micromachining of electrodes, and magnetoelectrochemistry.

Her group works at the intersection of biochemistry, analytical chemistry, and nanotechnology. Core themes include molecularly imprinted polymers (MIPs) as synthetic receptors, affinity cryogel columns for biochromatography, QCM and potentiometric biosensors, 3D-printable bioinks, and bioinspired nanoenzymes for catalysis and CO₂ conversion.

She co-founded Bionkit in 2012 as a commercial spin-off from Anadolu University, and currently serves as its CEO. She has held the position of Head of Biochemistry since 2010, and has been at ESTÜ since 2018, following appointments at Anadolu University (2001–2018).

Research
⬡

Molecular Imprinting

Design of MIPs as synthetic receptors for selective recognition of metal ions, biomolecules, pathogens, drugs, and environmental contaminants, using QCM, SPR, and chromatographic readouts.

◈

Biosensors & Nanosensors

QCM, SPR, and potentiometric platforms for clinical biomarkers — cancer antigens (CA125, PSA), autoimmune markers (citrulline, anti-CCP), and oxidative stress indicators (8-OHdG).

⬜

Biochromatography & Cryogels

Affinity cryogel columns and monolithic supports for protein, enzyme, and antibody purification; FPLC-integrated systems for hyaluronic acid, IgG, and hemoglobin isolation.

△

3D Bioprinting & Bioinks

Cellulose- and hydrogel-based bioinks incorporating photo-crosslinkable and self-healing polymers for scaffold fabrication, patches, and organ models for tissue engineering and drug delivery.

○

Nanoenzymes & Biomimetics

Synthetic enzyme systems — nanolipases, carbonic anhydrase mimics, and RuBisCO nanoenzymes — for biocatalysis and CO₂ conversion via photo-crosslinked polymer architectures.

◇

Electrochemistry & Conducting Polymers

Electropolymerization of polypyrrole and polyparaphenylene; overoxidized polymer electrodes; magnetoelectrochemistry; ion-selective electrodes; spectroelectrochemistry.

Spin-off Company
Bionkit
CEO & Co-founder · Founded 2012 · Anadolu University Spin-off · ATAP / ETGB Eskişehir

Bionkit was co-founded in 2012 by Prof. Arzu Ersöz and Prof. Rıdvan Say as a commercial spin-off of Anadolu University, based at the Eskişehir Technology Development Zone (ETGB). Prof. Ersöz currently serves as CEO, leading the company's mission to translate molecularly imprinted polymer and biochromatography research into commercial diagnostic and separation products and services. Bionkit received the Most Successful Woman Entrepreneur 3rd Place award from ETGB–ATAP in 2023.
